=== Slide 37 ===
Gotchas
Tagging becomes a security boundary
If someone can change tags, they can change access
Wrong or missing tags cause over/under access
Data can become exposed/blocked w/ no obvious error
Policy sprawl still happens
ABAC reduces grants, but high policy count is also hard to manage, e.g., if each team invents custom attributes
Mixed models can create surprises
ABAC grant policies can be superseded by regular grants due to permissions UNION
Performance and evaluation complexity
Large numbers of policies and conditions can slow authorization checks.
Attribute-based access controls
→
Tighten who can set attributes and audit changes, use RBAC
→
Add validation checks and fallback rules, e.g. by use “not verified” tags
→
Standardize attributes and naming and put in place as small of a permission set as you can
→
Avoid mixing without clear guidelines
→
Keep conditions simple and reusable
